\chapter{ABSTRACT}

\begin{singlespace}
\noindent
Off-road autonomous navigation presents significant challenges due to unstructured terrain, varying elevations, and dense vegetation. Traditional two-dimensional path planning algorithms often struggle to ensure platform safety and traversal efficiency in these complex, three-dimensional environments. This thesis proposes a novel terrain-aware path planning framework that integrates a localized 2.5D topology representation with smooth curve refinement to enable safe and optimal navigation. The core methodology introduces a modified A* search algorithm featuring a multi-variant Step Traversal Cost (STC) formulation. This STC heavily penalizes steep inclines---tunable via an adjustable slope weight parameter ($\lambda$)---and vegetation interference, effectively routing the vehicle along mathematically safe contours. To bridge the gap between grid-based discrete search and dynamically feasible vehicle execution, a specialized B-spline refinement layer is introduced. This post-processing layer iteratively smoothens the trajectory while strictly sampling underlying slope gradients to guarantee that the final curve does not violate the physical climbability thresholds of the vehicle. Extensive simulation across standardized synthetic maps (low-gradient, medium-relief, and high-relief terrains) demonstrates the robustness of the framework. Quantitative results indicate that the B-spline refinement reduces total spatial path length by 5.06--6.28\%, travel-time proxy by 4.98--5.40\%, and mean turn angle by 87.98--89.03\% compared to the raw A* output. Notably, these optimizations were achieved with zero slope violations and incurred less than 13 milliseconds of additional computational overhead, thereby confirming its suitability for real-time field deployment. The proposed framework significantly advances off-road autonomous navigation by explicitly balancing topological safety constraints with route optimality and continuous dynamic feasibility.
\end{singlespace}
